General Information

North Reef Island is an island of the Andaman Islands. It belongs to the North and Middle Andaman administrative district, part of the Indian union territory of Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

Geography

The island lies 156 km (97 mi) north from Port Blair.It lies west of North Andaman.

Fauna

North Reef Sanctuary is located on the island. it is dedicated to the nurturing of a variety of water birds.

Wildlife

North Reef Island is a part of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, which are known for their rich biodiversity and unique wildlife. It is likely that there are various forms of wildlife present on North Reef Island. Some of the common species found in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands include:

  1. Saltwater Crocodile: The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are home to one of the largest populations of saltwater crocodiles in the world.
  2. Giant Robber Crab: These crabs are known to grow up to three feet in length and are considered the largest terrestrial arthropod in the world.
  3. Dugongs: Dugongs are marine mammals that are found in the waters around the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  4. Nicobar Pigeon: The Nicobar Pigeon is a beautiful bird that is native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  5. Andaman Wild Pig: These wild pigs are found in the forests of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
It is important to note that North Reef Island is a protected area, and visitors are not allowed to disturb or harm the wildlife on the island.
