General Information

North Bay Island is known for its stunning coral reefs and clear waters, which offer excellent opportunities for snorkeling and diving. Visitors can explore the underwater world and observe a variety of marine life such as colorful fishes, sea turtles, and even sharks.

History

North Bay Island, also known as Coral Island or Mask Island, has a rich history that dates back to the British colonial era in India. The island was discovered by the British in the late 18th century and was initially used as a military outpost due to its strategic location in the Bay of Bengal.

During World War II, the island was occupied by the Japanese, who built several bunkers and gun emplacements on the island as part of their defensive strategy. The island was later retaken by the British in 1945, and many of the bunkers and gun emplacements can still be seen on the island today.

After India gained independence from British colonial rule in 1947, the Andaman and Nicobar Islands became a part of the Indian Union. North Bay Island was subsequently developed as a tourist destination due to its natural beauty and unique marine life. Today, the island attracts thousands of visitors every year who come to explore its coral reefs and enjoy various water activities.

In recent years, efforts have been made to promote eco-tourism on the island while also preserving the marine ecosystem. North Bay Island is home to a diverse array of wildlife, both on land and in the surrounding waters. The island is located within the Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park, which is a protected area that encompasses a range of different habitats, including coral reefs, mangroves, and tropical forests.
